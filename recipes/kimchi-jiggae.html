Kimchi Jiggae

Description

Kimchi jjigae (also spelled kimchi jigae) is a flavorful and hearty stew from Korea. The dish is made with bite-size pieces of kimchi, pork, onion, garlic, and other seasonings. Itâ€™s a warm, spicy, and savory dish that is sure to please almost everyone who can handle a bit of spice. To make a delicious kimchi jjigae, you will need the following ingredients: sesame oil, yellow onion, garlic, aged kimchi, mirin, rice cakes, young kimchi, stock or broth, and pork belly.

First, marinate the pork belly with the rice wine and the ground black pepper for about 15 mins. Then, cook the kimchi in a skillet until soft. Next, heat the sesame oil in a soup pot over medium-high heat and add the onion, garlic, and pork belly and cook for about 5 minutes. After that, add the aged kimchi, mirin, rice cakes, and young kimchi and mix it all together. Finally, add the stock or broth, cover the pot, and simmer for about 20 minutes.

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 1/2 yellow onion sliced
  • 2 cloves garlic minced
  • 1 cup aged kimchi cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 tsp mirin
  • 1 cup sliced rice cakes
  • 8 cups young (around 2-week old) kimchi
  • 8 cup stock or broth (either meat or vegetable)
  • 6 tablespoons mirin
  • 1/2 pound pork shoulder (or pork belly)

Steps

  1. Marinate the pork belly with the rice wine and the ground black pepper for about 15 mins.
  2. Cook the kimchi in a skillet until soft.
  3. Heat the sesame oil in a soup pot over medium-high heat and add the onion, garlic, and pork belly and cook for about 5 minutes.
  4. Add the aged kimchi, mirin, rice cakes, and young kimchi and mix it all together.
  5. Add the stock or broth, cover the pot, and simmer for about 20 minutes.
