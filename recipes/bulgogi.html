Bulgogi

Description

Bulgogi is a popular Korean BBQ dish made with thinly sliced pieces of beef marinated in a savory mixture of soy sauce, garlic, sesame oil, brown sugar, and other ingredients.

It is traditionally cooked over a charcoal fire, although it can also be cooked in a pan. The thin slices of beef cook quickly, so there is no need to marinate it for long periods of time.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup (120ml) soy sauce
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons (20ml) gochujang (spicy Korean red pepper paste)
  • 3 large cloves garlic, grated or minced
  • 1/2 Asian pear (or 1/2 red apple)
  • 2 tablespoons (30ml) honey
  • 2 tablespoons (30ml) sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons (30ml) sugar
  • 2 tablespoons (30ml) minced green onion
  • 2 tablespoons (30ml) roasted sesame seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper

Steps

  1. Mix all the marinade ingredients in a bowl and set aside.
  2. Thinly slice the beef and place it in the bowl with the marinade. Make sure the beef is completely covered in the sauce.
  3. Cover the bowl and let the beef marinate for at least 30 minutes, up to overnight.
  4. Heat up a large skillet or grill pan over medium heat and add a tablespoon of oil.
  5. Add the marinated beef to the pan and cook for 2-3 minutes per side, or until it is cooked through.
  6. Serve with steamed rice, kimchi, and other Korean side dishes. Enjoy!
