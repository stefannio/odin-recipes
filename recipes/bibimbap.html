Bibimbap

Description

To make traditional bibimbap, prepare and cook the ingredients. Start by mixing the beef mince with the meat sauce. Then, add the vegetables such as mushrooms, soybean sprouts, spinach, zucchini, cucumber, tofu, bellflower root, lettuce, and carrots to the mix. Put the rice into a bowl and add all of the prepared ingredients.

To make the bibimbap even more flavorful, top it off with a fried egg, gochujang (chili pepper paste), soy sauce, or doenjang (fermented soybean paste). Once all the ingredients are in the bowl, mix them together thoroughly. With lots of mix-and-match options and tasks of varying difficulty levels, this bibimbap recipe is perfect for making with kids of all ages. Enjoy your delicious bibimbap!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups medium-grain Korean rice, or Japanese rice
  • 1 large cucumber, sliced into thin strips
  • 1 1/2 cups spinach, parboiled and drained
  • 1/2 teaspoon rice vinegar
  • 1 1/4 teaspoons sesame oil, divided
  • 1 cup fresh mung bean sprouts
  • 1 cup shredded carrots
  • 4 ounces partially frozen beef, thinly sliced
  • 1/4 cup gochujang (Korean hot pepper paste)
  • 1 egg

Steps

  1. Cook the rice according to your preferred method.
  2. Prepare and cook the ingredients as desired, such as steaming and blanching vegetables, slicing and salting vegetables, and saut√©ing vegetables and chiles.
  3. Arrange the cooked ingredients in a bowl.
  4. Top with a raw egg yolk and gochujang (Korean hot pepper paste).
  5. Mix everything together before eating. Enjoy!
